<h2 id="projects" class="section-heading">Research Projects</h2>

<div class="project-card">
  <h3>Vocabulary Acquisition in Noisy Social Contexts</h3>
  <p>
    Ongoing work examining how auditory noise and social contexts (child-directed, overheard, audio-only) shape word learning and long-term retention in autistic children.
    This line of work asks how ecologically valid learning contexts alter vocabulary acquisition and retention.
  </p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Social learning:</strong> tests how face visibility and auditory noise affect word learning in neurotypical and neurodivergent children.</li>
    <li><strong>Implicit learning:</strong> examines whether music entrainment enhances children's statistical learning using behavioral and EEG measures.</li>
    <li><strong>Developmental pathways:</strong> investigates links among multisensory integration, social cognition, and language learning across development.</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="mini-meta">Behavioral</p>
</div>

<div class="project-card">
  <h3>Statistical Learning Across Development</h3>
  <p>
    My dissertation work examined how language, attention, and neural systems interact during statistical learning in children and adults,
    and whether these mechanisms differ across typical and atypical language development.
  </p>
  <p class="mini-meta">Behavioral + fMRI + EEG</p>
</div>

<div class="project-card">
  <h3>Reading Impairment and Incidental Learning</h3>
  <p>
    I also study incidental language learning in children with reading difficulties. I examine whether reading personalized stories in commercial apps facilitate incidental word learning and long-term reading interests in children with reading impairment. I also examined whether implicit word-form learning via statistical learning could predict reading intervention gains in longitudinal studies.
  </p>
  <p class="mini-meta">Behavioral (longitudinal)</p>
</div>

<div class="project-card">
  <h3>Speech Adaptation and Prosody in Autism</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Examines pitch imitation and prosodic adaptation in school-aged autistic children.</li>
    <li>Includes open-source tools for remote speech data collection and analysis.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="project-card">
  <h3>Online Methods for Developmental Research</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Builds child-friendly online games and experimental pipelines for remote data collection.</li>
    <li>Develops efficient coding and analysis workflows for eye-tracking, transcription, and behavioral data.</li>
  </ul>
</div>
